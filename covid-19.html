Covid-19


What is it?

First identified in Wuhan, China, Covid-19 is an infectious disease which has spread across the whole world. It was born in December 19th, 2019 which is why it is called COVID-19. It was declared a pandemic on March 11th, 2020. Another name for Covid-19 is CoronaVirus.

The crown like spikes on the surface of the Covid-19 cell (red in this case) are actually protein spikes used to access host cells in our body to reproduce. These protein spikes attach to host cells and the cells pull them in. Then the RNA (purple in this case), sort of like the DNA of the Covid-19 cell which is inside, passes into the host cell and makes it produce all the materials needed for a new Covid-19 cell. The Covid-19 cell has a fatty layer (orange in this case) protecting the RNA and helps to get inside a host cell.
